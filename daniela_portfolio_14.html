Eastman Chemical Company — Full Financial Analysis
EMN · NYSE · Specialty Chemicals · 2019–2023
A comprehensive five-year financial analysis of Eastman Chemical Company (EMN), structured across four interconnected analytical modules: financial performance, financial risk, cost of capital, and total share yield. Working from raw GAAP financial statements, I reconstructed the full income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow classification framework — then derived all ratios, aggregates, and valuations from first principles using Excel.
Coverage2019 · 2020 · 2021 · 2022 · 2023
Revenue Range$8.47B – $10.58B
ToolsExcel (Advanced) · CAPM · WACC · DuPont
Deliverables4 Excel workbooks · Written analysis
Download Excel Workbook
Key Analytical Findings
ROIC nearly doubled from 5.8% (2020) to 10.34% (2023), demonstrating improving capital efficiency post-pandemic — but productivity metrics (ICTR, inventory days) deteriorated, with DIO exceeding COVID-era levels at 91 days.
2023 EBIT was materially inflated by €323M in non-operating gains. Stripping these out revealed that core operating profitability was considerably weaker — a finding obscured by headline figures.
Beta remained elevated post-COVID (1.57 in 2023 vs. 1.40 in 2019), driving Cost of Equity to 17.64% in 2023. WACC dipped to 5.70% in 2022 exclusively due to a collapse in market return — not operational improvement.
Total Share Yield turned negative in 2022 (–$25.23 capital loss) and underperformed Ke in 2023. A ~50% growth in treasury shares from 2020–2023 masked what would otherwise have been even weaker market returns.

EHL Student Career Survey — Descriptive Analysis & Confidence Intervals
n = 127 · 22 Variables · Prof. Petar Zivkovic · Team 10
Analysed survey data from 127 EHL students across 22 variables covering career aspirations, workplace preferences, skill priorities, and professional ambitions. Produced full descriptive statistics for each variable — including distributions, central tendency, IQR, and coefficient of variation — then constructed 95% confidence intervals for two continuous variables and four proportions using manual Excel derivation.
Samplen = 127 EHL students · 22 survey variables
MethodsDescriptive stats · CI for μ and p · CV · Boxplots · Cross-tabulation
ToolsExcel · Manual CI derivation
CourseStatistics · EHL Hospitality Business School
Download Excel Workbook
Key Findings
Only 22.8% of EHL students plan a career in hospitality — 43.3% are "not sure" — striking for a hospitality-only school. Entrepreneurship dominates 10-year ambitions at 58.3%, with "own my own business" the most common goal.
Career motivation is split: 41.7% cite Career Opportunities, 29.9% Personal Interest. Financial success is the top achievement goal. Yet finance as a target sector is only 4.7% — suggesting aspiration-sector misalignment.
Students show wider consensus on countries willing to work in (CV 78.8%) than on networking event attendance (CV 67.7%) — suggesting mobility is more individually defined than social strategy.
95% CI for mean age: [20.80, 21.38]. CI for "Big Corp" preference: [37.8%, 55.1%]. CI for income goal ≥€200k: [12.8%, 26.6%] — wide interval signalling genuine ambition variance across the cohort.
